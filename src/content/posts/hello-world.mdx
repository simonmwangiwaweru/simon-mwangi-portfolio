Most of what I build starts from a real constraint, not a tutorial. Kazi Mtaani exists because informal workers in Kenya often don't have a smartphone or reliable data — so a job marketplace that only works as an app quietly excludes the people who need it most. That's the kind of problem I want to keep working on.

## What to expect here

This blog is where I'll write about the projects in more depth than a case study allows — the integration decisions, the parts that broke, and what I'd do differently. Expect posts on:

- Building USSD flows with Africa's Talking
- Wiring up M-Pesa Daraja for escrow-style payments
- Where AI genuinely helps a small business, and where it's just noise

## Why now

I'm final-year at JKUAT, increasingly focused on AI/ML engineering on top of full-stack work, and I'd rather document the process than polish a highlight reel after the fact.

More soon.
